24-6-2018 Phoenix Coyotes trade
Phoenix Coyotes traded Brayden McNabb and Marc-Andre Fleury to St. Louis Blues for Alexander Steen, Robbie Fabbri, Jay Bouwmeester, Tage Thompson and Klim Kostin

A native of Canada, Alexander Steen was originally drafted to the NHL by the Toronto Maple Leafs in the 1st round of the 2002 entry draft. He has appeared in 815 NHL games during his career, notching 219 goals and adding 326 assists along with 385 penalty minutes. The 34 year old left wing is waiting for a good start this season.

A native of Canada, Robbie Fabbri was originally drafted to the NHL by the St. Louis Blues in the 1st round of the 2014 entry draft. He has appeared in 141 NHL games during his career, notching 26 goals and adding 32 assists along with 73 penalty minutes. The 22 year old center is looking forward for the start of the season.

A native of Canada, Jay Bouwmeester was originally drafted to the NHL by the Florida Panthers in the 1st round of the 2002 entry draft. He has appeared in 1072 NHL games during his career, notching 85 goals and adding 305 assists along with 545 penalty minutes. The 34 year old defenseman is looking forward for the start of the season.

A native of USA, Tage Thompson was originally drafted to the NHL by the St. Louis Blues in the 1st round of the 2016 entry draft. He has not yet played at NHL level. The 20 year old center is expecting a good season for the team.

A native of Russia, Klim Kostin was originally drafted to the NHL by the New York Rangers in the 1st round of the 2017 entry draft. He has not yet played at NHL level. The 19 year old right wing is getting ready for the upcoming season.

A native of Canada, Brayden McNabb was originally drafted to the NHL by the Buffalo Sabres in the 3rd round of the 2009 entry draft. He has appeared in 271 NHL games during his career, notching 7 goals and adding 66 assists along with 199 penalty minutes. The 27 year old defenseman is expecting a good season for the team.

A native of Canada, Marc-Andre Fleury was originally drafted to the NHL by the Pittsburgh Penguins in the 1st round of the 2003 entry draft. He has appeared in 700 NHL games during his career, booking 45 career shutouts and allowing 1726 goals. The 33 year old goaltender is expecting a good season for the team.

20-6-2018 David Perron frustrated
Vegas Golden Knights left wing David Perron feels frustrated about his position in the organization. He feels he's good enough to be a core player for the team and wonders why the organization still considers him a minor league player.

A native of Canada, David Perron was originally drafted to the NHL by the St. Louis Blues in the 1st round of the 2007 entry draft. He has appeared in 652 NHL games during his career, notching 155 goals and adding 217 assists along with 520 penalty minutes. The 30 year old left wing has played 82 games with the Vegas Golden Knights this season, totalling 14+26=40 with 74 PIM.
